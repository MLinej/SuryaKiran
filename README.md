Problem Statement
Solar power plants rely on inverters to convert the electricity generated by photovoltaic (PV) panels into usable AC power. When an inverter experiences degradation, malfunction, or sudden failure, the plant’s power generation capacity drops significantly.
Currently, most solar plants detect inverter issues only after they occur through alarms and monitoring dashboards. By the time operators notice the problem, energy production has already been lost, resulting in:
Reduced energy generation
Lower plant performance ratio (PR)
Increased downtime 
Significant financial losses for operators
The challenge is to develop a system that can predict potential inverter failures or underperformance within a 7–10 day window and provide actionable insights so operators can intervene before energy loss occurs.
Current Gap in Technology
Despite advancements in solar monitoring systems, most existing solutions focus on reactive monitoring rather than predictive intelligence.
Key limitations of current systems include:
1. Reactive Monitoring
Most dashboards only show real-time metrics and alerts after failures occur, rather than predicting issues in advance.
2. Lack of Explainability
Even when anomalies are detected, operators often receive raw numerical data without clear explanations about the root cause.
3. Limited Decision Support
Current platforms rarely provide actionable maintenance recommendations, forcing operators to manually diagnose issues.
4. No Business Impact Visibility
Existing monitoring tools do not quantify energy loss or financial impact, making it difficult for operators to prioritize maintenance actions.
5. Fragmented Systems
Telemetry monitoring, predictive analytics, and maintenance management are often handled by separate systems, reducing operational efficiency.
Our Solution
We propose an AI-Driven Solar Inverter Intelligence Platform that combines machine learning, data analytics, and generative AI to provide predictive insights for solar plant operators.
Our platform continuously analyzes inverter telemetry data to detect patterns that indicate potential failures or performance degradation.
The system provides three key capabilities:
1. Predictive Failure Detection
Using machine learning models trained on inverter telemetry data, the system predicts the probability that an inverter will fail or underperform within the next 7–10 days.
2. Explainable AI Insights
The system integrates explainability techniques (such as SHAP feature importance) and generative AI to produce clear, natural language explanations describing why an inverter is at risk and what factors contribute to the prediction.
3. Intelligent Operational Dashboard
Operators can monitor plant performance through an interactive dashboard that provides:
Per-inverter risk scores
Telemetry trend visualizations
AI-generated operational summaries
Alert notifications
Maintenance recommendations
This enables operators to proactively address issues before energy loss occurs, improving plant reliability and efficiency.
Unique Selling Point (USP)
Our platform goes beyond simple failure prediction by translating technical insights into business-level impact analysis.
⚡ Energy and Revenue Loss Prediction
In addition to predicting inverter failures, the system estimates:
Potential energy loss (kWh)
Estimated revenue loss
Preventable savings through early intervention
By quantifying the financial impact of potential failures, operators can prioritize maintenance actions based on both technical risk and economic impact.
This feature transforms the system from a monitoring tool into a decision-support platform for solar plant management.

Models Used

This project uses a combination of Machine Learning, Explainable AI, and Generative AI to predict solar inverter failures and generate operational insights.

1. Failure Prediction Model

We use XGBoost, a powerful gradient boosting algorithm designed for tabular data, to predict whether a solar inverter is likely to fail or underperform within the next 7–10 days.

2. Model Explainability

To make the predictions interpretable, SHAP (SHapley Additive Explanations) is used to identify the key features contributing to inverter risk, such as temperature rise or power output drop.

3. Anomaly Detection

An Isolation Forest model is used as an additional layer to detect abnormal inverter behavior and unusual telemetry patterns.

4. Generative AI

A Large Language Model (Gemini / GPT) is used to generate natural language explanations of model predictions and suggest recommended maintenance actions.
