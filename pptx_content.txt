PROBLEM STATEMENT



AI-Driven
Solar Inverter Failure Prediction & Intelligence Platform

To predict the likelihood that an solar inverter will experience a shutdown or significant underperformance within a 7–10-day prediction window.

Generate natural language operational summaries and root-cause hypotheses using Generative AI

Business Context



Solar power plants use inverters to convert electricity from solar panels into usable power. If an inverter stops working or performs poorly, the plant produces less electricity. This leads to:

Loss of energy generation

Reduced performance ratio (PR)

Lower uptime

Financial loss for the company


Business Context



Right now, most solar plants detect inverter problems after they happen using alarms and dashboards. By the time operators notice the issue, some energy has already been lost.

Your solution should improve this by providing two key capabilities:

Predictive Visibility
Use Machine Learning to estimate the risk that an inverter might fail or underperform in the next 7–10 days.
This helps operators act before energy loss happens.

Generative Insight
Use Generative AI to explain:
Why the inverter is at risk
What might be causing the issue
What actions operators should take
Instead of just showing numbers, the system should provide clear, easy-to-understand guidance


DETAILS



Your ML pipeline must demonstrate production-readiness, not just exploratory analysis.

Minimum Requirements
Binary classification (failure / no failure) or risk score regression within the prediction window.
Feature engineering from telemetry, computed KPIs, and alarm data.
Cross-validation and hold-out evaluation with precision, recall, F1, and AUC reported.
Model explainability: SHAP values or LIME for at least the top 5 contributing features.

Bonus
Time-series aware splits (e.g., walk-forward validation).
Anomaly detection as a complementary signal layer.
Multi-class output: no risk/degradation risk/shutdown risk.
